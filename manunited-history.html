History

  • Newton Heath L&YR Football Club was founded, the club that later would become Manchester United FC.

    1878

  • They played in the Football League (Premier League) for the very first time but were relegated after 2 years.

    1892

  • New ownership took over and renamed the club Manchester United.

    1902

  • Won their first Football League title.

    1908

  • Won their first FA Cup title.

    1909

  • Old Trafford officially opened on 19 February 1910 with a match between Manchester and Liverpool (3-4).

    1910

  • Matt Busby was appointed manager. He reshaped the club and placed a new faith in youngsters.

    1945

  • Became the youngest side ever to win the Football League which earned them the nickname the “Busby Babes”.

    1956

  • The Munich Air Disaster was the darkest day in Manchester United History. On a flight back to England, the team’s plane crashed. Eight players and three members of the staff died.


    The eight players who perished were Geoff Bent (aged 25), Roger Byrne (28), Eddie Colman (21), Duncan Edwards (21), Mark Jones (24), David Pegg (22), Tommy Taylor (26), and Liam Whelan (22). Edwards, considered by many to be the finest player of his generation, died 15 days after the accident.


    The three club officials who were killed were secretary Walter Crickmer, trainer Tom Curry and coach Bert Whalley.

    1958

  • Alex Ferguson was appointed manager.

    1986

  • Ferguson’s first few years as manager were difficult until the team won the FA Cup in 1990. Over the next twenty years, the club won at least 25 major trophies, including around eleven League titles.

    1990

  • Manchester United PLC is listed on the London Stock Exchange.

    1991

  • Became the first English club to win a Treble while winning the FA Cup, the Premier League, and UEFA Champions League simultaneously during 1998-1999.

    1999

  • First-time Europa League winners.

    2017
